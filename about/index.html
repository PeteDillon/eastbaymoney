"In Oregon, the model of excellence is a scientist and a runner. I tried to take something from both."

I grew up in Oregon, where two native sons cast long shadows. Linus Pauling — Portland-born, two-time Nobel laureate — brought systematic rigor to chemistry the way a craftsman brings care to a joint: nothing sloppy, nothing left unexplained. Steve Prefontaine ran every race from the front, turned suffering into strategy, and said that to give anything less than your best is to sacrifice the gift.

A scientist and a runner. In Oregon, those felt like the same kind of person — and the same kind of mind.

That's the spirit I've tried to carry into finance and tax work. I studied chemistry at Macalester, then came to Berkeley for a graduate degree in chemistry and an MBA at Haas, then spent a career at the intersection of finance, tax, and operations — from early-stage tech companies to the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, where I lead FP&A for the Immunization team in the Global Development division. I'm a licensed California CPA (#159626). And I still run, ski, climb, and ride — because the discipline of the long effort, and beginning always with the end in mind, turns out to be just as useful at a desk.
