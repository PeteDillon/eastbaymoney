About Pete

From the natural world
to the human one

"Physics and chemistry explain much of the physical world. Economics, finance, and accounting explain much of the human world. I've spent my career learning both."

I grew up drawn to systems — the kind of rigorous, internally consistent frameworks that actually explain why things work the way they do. That impulse took me first into chemistry: a degree at Macalester, a graduate degree in atmospheric chemistry at UC Berkeley, a year at 3M. Precise, explanatory, deeply satisfying.

Finance pulled me in through a side door. Working at Chevron in environmental consulting, I found myself embedded in their corporate accounting function, reporting energy metrics to the board. I admired the discipline. I read Titan — Ron Chernow's biography of Rockefeller, who trained as an accountant — and saw the same rigor I loved in science applied to human organizations. I went back to Berkeley for an MBA at Haas and a certificate in accounting through Berkeley Extension, with a mentor, Richard Long, a seasoned Silicon Valley CFO, who showed me how powerful the role of finance can be in building great organizations.

What followed was a career at the intersection of finance, tax, and operations — from early-stage tech companies to the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, where I currently lead FP&A partnership to the Immunization team in the Global Development division. I'm working toward my CPA license. And I'm spending this spring in the East Bay doing what I've always done: trying to understand how things work — and helping people use that understanding to make better decisions.
