A bit about me

I started out in the physical sciences — chemistry at Macalester, a graduate degree in atmospheric chemistry from Berkeley, a year as a chemist at 3M. I loved the rigor: the idea that careful observation and honest evidence could explain how the world actually works.

Finance got its hooks in me when I realized it offers the same thing, just for human behavior. I went back to Berkeley for an MBA at Haas, earned an accounting certificate through Berkeley Extension, and have spent the years since at the intersection of finance, tax, and operations — from early-stage tech to the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.
